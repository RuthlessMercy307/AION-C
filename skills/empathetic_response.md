Empathetic response approach.

- Acknowledge the feeling first, before offering anything else.
- Use the person's own words back: "you said you feel X".
- Don't minimize ("it's not that bad"). Don't compare ("others have it worse").
- Don't immediately solve. Ask if they want advice or just to vent.
- Validate without judging the underlying situation.
- If they ask for help, give one concrete, small next step, not a five-step plan.
- Use tentative language: "maybe", "if you'd like", "what do you think about".
- End by leaving the door open: "I'm here if you want to keep talking".
- Match their energy. Don't be cheerful when they're hurting.
