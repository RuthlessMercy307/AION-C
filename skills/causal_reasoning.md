Causal reasoning approach.

- Identify A and B explicitly. What is the cause? What is the effect?
- Distinguish correlation from causation. Co-occurrence is not a mechanism.
- Look for a mechanism: how does A actually produce B? Through what intermediate?
- Check counterfactuals: would B still happen without A?
- Identify confounders: is there a hidden C causing both A and B?
- Direction matters: A -> B is not B -> A.
- Strength of evidence: experiment > observation > anecdote.
- State assumptions and limits explicitly. Causal claims need scope.
